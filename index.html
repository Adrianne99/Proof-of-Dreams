FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

An ERC20 token is a standard used for creating and issuing smart contracts on the Ethereum blockchain. Smart contracts can then be used to create smart property or tokenized assets that people can invest in. ERC stands for "Ethereum request for comment," and the ERC20 standard was implemented in 2015.

When trading on Decentralized Exchanges (DEX), such as Uniswap, in order to not always look for a counterpart who would like to make the same trade in reverse, traders trade against a liquidity pool so that they can trade instantaneously. In many projects, the team can instantaneously remove the liquidity pool, thereby “rugging,” or “rugpulling” the investors. This liquidity pool being locked by a third party limits the team’s access to the liquidity pool, thereby ensuring that holders and traders can always continue to trade the tokens smoothly.
